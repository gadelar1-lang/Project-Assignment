Experience



Hello! Thank you for stopping by.

I started on the retail floor and stayed because I loved the work people do every day. Over 21 years I moved from frontline roles into leadership, carrying one conviction with me: when people are supported, trained, and trusted, they do extraordinary work. That belief guided my time at Sam’s Club and Rooms To Go and now shapes how I lead in Rooms to Go while running De La Rosa Realty. My career is measured not only in P&L and sales figures but in the leaders I helped grow and the teams that learned to solve problems together.

Mentoring Philosophy People first. I lead by listening before prescribing. I believe mentorship is a two-way conversation where curiosity, patience, and honest feedback create space for growth. Growth over perfection. I focus on progress—small wins, repeated—rather than one-off perfection. This reduces fear of failure and encourages experimentation. Contextual coaching. Training must match the moment: quick, practical coaching on the floor; structured development plans for aspiring managers; and strategic mentorship for leaders preparing to run a facility.


How I Develop Others

• One-on-one coaching with clear, actionable next steps and follow-up to track progress.
• Shadowing and stretch assignments that let associates learn by doing while still having a safety net.
• Structured training programs I designed as a Store Manager Trainer and Regional Mentor to standardize best practices while leaving room for individual strengths.
• Regular feedback loops that combine data (KPIs, P&L, safety metrics) with human observations so coaching is both measurable and meaningful.
• Recognition and celebration of milestones—promotions, safety improvements, customer service wins—to reinforce behaviors and build confidence.



Concrete Examples

• Built leaders from within. I mentored associates into supervisory and management roles, creating a pipeline that improved retention and reduced hiring costs.
• Safety culture through coaching. By coaching frontline leaders on near‑miss reporting and root‑cause thinking, we cut accident costs dramatically and made safety a shared responsibility.
• Operational ownership. I coached teams to own P&L outcomes by teaching them how daily decisions affect margins, which led to sustained profitability improvements across multiple clubs.




Core Principles

• Empathy and Respect — Treat every person as a whole person, not just a role.
• Psychological Safety — Create environments where people can speak up, make mistakes, and learn.
• Clarity and Accountability — Set clear expectations and support people to meet them.
• Continuous Learning — Use Lean Six Sigma and 5S as tools for everyday improvement, not just projects.
• Servant Leadership — My job is to remove obstacles so others can succeed.
